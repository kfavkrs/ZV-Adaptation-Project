# BMO Three-Dimension Adaptation Timeline — Expectation vs. Example

**Source:** Synthesized from `adaptation-research-framework.md`, drawing specifically on the Black, Mendenhall & Oddou (1991) `[Academic]` three-dimension adjustment model — self-oriented, others-oriented, and perceptual adjustment. Timeline anchors come from Domain 1 (Social & Community) and Domain 2 (Emotional & Psychological); perceptual timing is synthesized from the cognition/perception notes woven into each stage, since BMO's perceptual dimension isn't given its own dedicated arc in the source document.

**Date:** 2026-07-10

---

| Stage | Internal resources — expectation | Internal resources — example | Relationship building — expectation | Relationship building — example | Cultural perception — expectation | Cultural perception — example |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Honeymoon (weeks 2–8) | Confidence intact; friction reads as adventure | Learning the OV-chipkaart, bike parking, the weekly Albert Heijn rhythm | Surface acquaintances form easily | Colleagues, neighbors, people met at expat events | Directness reads as refreshingly honest | No reframing needed yet |

**Cross-cutting note:** Month 6 is flagged in the source as the convergence point where all three dimensions hit hardest simultaneously — peak culture shock, worst social loneliness, first Dutch winter, language frustration. Highest-leverage moment to plan around explicitly.
