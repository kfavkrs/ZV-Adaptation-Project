# Cultural Adaptation Framework Survey

**Decision:** Composite framework (see below)
**Date:** 2026-05-26

---

## Frameworks Evaluated

### 1. Ward's ABC Model (Ward, Bochner & Furnham, 2001)
**Organizes around:** Affect (how you feel), Behavior (what skills you learn), Cognition (how you think about your identity)

- Maps directly to actionable domains — emotions to manage, skills to acquire, identity shifts to navigate
- Less prescriptive about *when* things happen — needs a timeline overlay
- Example: "Behavioral adaptation — Dutch social scripts: the birthday circle (*verjaardagskring*), direct feedback norms, and the unspoken rules of *gezelligheid*."

**Source:** [Ward's ABC Framework](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/370979177_From_Cultural_Shock_to_ABC_Framework_Development_of_Intercultural_Contact_Theory)

### 2. Kim's Stress-Adaptation-Growth Dynamic (Kim, 2001)
**Organizes around:** A spiral cycle — stress triggers learning, learning creates growth, growth creates new stress at a higher level. Communication competence as the engine.

- Captures the non-linear reality — you cycle through discomfort and breakthrough repeatedly
- More theoretical/abstract; harder to turn into discrete activities
- Example: "Cycle 3 (months 4-6): Stress from realizing polite Dutch ≠ friendly Dutch triggers deeper social learning → growth in reading indirect social cues."

**Sources:**
- [Kim's Integrative Communication Theory](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1002/9781118783665.ieicc0041)
- [Kim's Stress-Adaptation-Growth Dynamic](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/373242267_Stress-Adaptation-Growth_Dynamic)

### 3. Black, Mendenhall & Oddou's Expatriate Adjustment Model (1991)
**Organizes around:** Four dimensions — self-oriented (confidence, self-regulation), others-oriented (relationship-building skills), perceptual (understanding why locals act as they do), cultural toughness (how different the new culture is).

- Built specifically for expatriates (not students or refugees)
- Self/others/perceptual split is practical for a couple — can assess yourselves on each dimension
- Originally designed for corporate expats; "cultural toughness" dimension is more macro comparison than personal action
- Example: "Others-oriented: Dutch social circles close slowly — relationships form through repeated, low-key encounters, not one big dinner."

**Source:** [Black, Mendenhall & Oddou — Expatriate Adjustment Framework](https://journals.aom.org/doi/10.5465/amr.1991.4278938)

### 4. Berry's Acculturation Framework (1997)
**Organizes around:** Four strategies — integration, assimilation, separation, marginalization.

- Useful as a one-time strategy declaration — we're pursuing integration
- Three of four quadrants are irrelevant to our situation
- **Omitted from composite** — integration strategy is already understood

### 5. Oberg's U-Curve / W-Curve (1954/1963)
**Organizes around:** Temporal stages — honeymoon → crisis → recovery → adjustment.

- The timeline layer — approximate emotional arc with rough month markers
- One-dimensional — only covers emotional state over time, not what to do about it
- Companion to a richer framework, not standalone

**Source:** [Theoretical Models of Culture Shock and Adaptation](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/03075070701794833)

---

## Composite Decision

**Primary structure:** Black, Mendenhall & Oddou's dimensions (self / others / perceptual) — practical, expat-specific, maps to actionable life domains

**Woven within each section:** Ward's ABC — distinguishes "what you feel" vs "what you do" vs "how you think" about each domain

**Meta-narrative:** Kim's spiral — the document acknowledges that adaptation cycles rather than proceeding linearly

**Temporal overlay:** U-Curve timeline — woven into each domain per the decision to do brief overview + domain-integrated timeline notes

**Omitted:** Berry — integration strategy is already understood

**Additional reference:**
- [Meta-Analytic Review of Expatriate Adjustment Predictors](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9449450/)
