# Visualizations

Three Economist-style charts derived from the research framework documents in this repo.

---

## zv_ucurve.png — The hardest months come after the honeymoon ends

The psychological arc of cross-cultural adaptation, plotted over 30 months. Based on Oberg's U-Curve with stage boundaries mapped to approximate month ranges. Nadir annotated at ~month 4; the 2.5-year meaningful-integration average (Americans in NL) marked as a reference line.

Sources: Oberg (1954); Black, Mendenhall & Oddou (1991); Kim (2001)

---

## zv_domains.png — Social integration is the last domain to yield

Dot chart showing when each of the 10 life domains peaks in difficulty across adaptation stages. The four domains that cluster in Culture Shock (social, language, emotional, daily life) are the primary design target for early-stage missions.

Sources: Black, Mendenhall & Oddou (1991); Ward, Bochner & Furnham (2001)

---

## zv_octalysis.png — Two motivation levers remain untapped

Scores the Alien Anthropologist gamification concept against Yu-kai Chou's 8 Octalysis core drives. Epic Meaning and Unpredictability are fully activated; Development and Social Influence are partial; Creativity & Feedback and Ownership & Possession are identified as design gaps to close in the next phase.

Sources: Yu-kai Chou, Octalysis Framework; game-design-survey.md
